Jamshedpur

Jamshedpur (Hindi: [d͡ʒəmˈʃeːd̪pʊɾ] ⓘ; Bengali: [ˈd͡ʒamʃedpuɾ]), also known as Tatanagar, is a major industrial city in eastern India.[7][8][9] Located on the confluence of Swarnarekha and Kharkai rivers, the city is surrounded by Dalma Hills.[10][11][12] It is the largest city in the state of Jharkhand. With a population of 629,658 in the city limits and 1.3 million in the wider metropolitan area, Jamshedpur is the third largest metropolitan area in the region and 36th largest urban area in the country and 72nd most populous city in the country.[13][14][15] The modern industrial city of Jamshedpur was built over the village of Sakchi by Dorabji Tata, who chose the location for his father Jamshedji Tata's planned steel city. The project began in 1908, with the steel plant operating by 1912, and the city was formally named Jamshedpur by Lord Chelmsford in 1919 in honor of Jamshedji.
